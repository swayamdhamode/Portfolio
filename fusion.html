My learning in 3D Design
from day 1 to day 8

Day 1.

Installation and SetupDownload and install Fusion 360 using your student ID.Set up your account and familiarize yourself with the student license features.Explore the interface and navigation tools within Fusion 360.

day 2.

Sketching FundamentalsLearn the basics of sketching by creating simple 2D shapes like lines, circles, and rectangles.Understand constraints and dimensions to fully define sketches.Practice sketching exercises to improve accuracy and speed.

day 3.

3D Modeling BasicsDive into 3D modeling by extruding your 2D sketches into 3D objects.Experiment with different modeling commands such as extrude, revolve, and sweep.Start conceptualizing simple objects or parts you'd like to create.

day 4.

Parametric DesignLearn about parametric modeling and how to use parameters and equations to drive your designs.Explore the timeline feature to understand how design history affects your model.Practice making design changes by modifying parameters.

day 5.

Assembly DesignExplore assembly design by creating multiple components and assembling them together.Learn about joints and constraints to simulate real-world connections between parts.Experiment with motion studies to visualize the functionality of your assemblies.

day 6.

Advanced Modeling TechniquesDive deeper into Fusion 360's advanced modeling tools such as fillet, chamfer, and loft.Practice creating more complex shapes and surfaces using these tools.Experiment with sculpting and organic modeling if interested.

day 7.

Rendering and VisualizationExplore Fusion 360's rendering workspace to create realistic renders of your designs.Learn how to apply materials, textures, and environments to enhance your renders.Experiment with lighting setups to create dramatic effects.

day 8.

Project Work and ReviewWork on a small project or design challenge to apply the skills you've learned.Review your progress over the past week and identify areas for improvement.Seek feedback from peers or online communities and iterate on your designs accordingly.
